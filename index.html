Welcome to Defi Domains

Welcome to DefiDomains, a fork of the ENS system supporting additional TLDs with ambitions of creating a full alternative root node system.

Right now DefiDomains only provides new TLDs, but future goals are to create an alternative to handshake and ICANN DNS built on the Ethereum blockchain.

We provide registration and resolution of decentralized domain names utilizing the Ethereum blockchain.

Purchase a Domain:

When purchasing a domain, you'll recieve two transactions. The first is a "commitment" to prevent front running. Once you submit this transaction, you'll wait for 1.5 minutes and then be presented with a second transaction where you will buy and register the domain.

This second transaction will include the domain fee +10% so that if the price of ETH changes while waiting for the transaction you'll still get the domain. The leftover ETH will be returned to you.


Status:

Domain names on the regular internet are managed by ICANN and DNS (the system for resolving domain names) is managed by their subsidiary IANA.

While previously ICANN was a federal entity with the US department of labor - it was spun out into a private non-profit foundation and promised to decentralize their control over domain name spaces. That never happened, instead they created and auctioned off hundreds of new TLDs and maintained tight control over DNS which runs on only 13 root node servers managed by IANA.

Alternative root node systems attempt to replace ICANN's hold on domain names. Projects like Namecoin and Handshake have tried to solve this on their own blockchains, and projects like the Ethereum Naming Service (which this project is based on) have tried to solve that challenge for specific name spaces. But, no one has attempted to build out broad, multi-namespace infastructure to replace the ICANN/DNS system and secure it with Ethereum.

DefiDomains provides users the ability to register decentralized (ERC721 NFT) domain names on unique name spaces like .defi .dapp and more than 35+ other TLDs slated for launch. We also allow users and companies to apply for and manage their own custom TLD.

DefiDomains was built on a fork of ENS a great open-source non-profit system which paved the foundation for Ethereum naming. As such we're compatible with most ENS based tooling. We plan to continue to support ENS by donating a portion of our revenue to their continued work.

Each TLD has a 'resolver' contract allowing it to resolve centralized websites, decentralized websites, wallet addresses, email addresses, Twitter handles, and whatever other content a user wishes to serve up. It also supports multi-context resolving, so the same address can point to a website when being typed into an address bar or a wallet address when typed into a wallet transaction. We even support multi-cointype lookups, meaning a user could type in their name as "defi.wallet" at an exchange when withdrawing crypto, and the exchange could see a list of wallets knowing which wallet to send to if the user is withdrawing BTC, and sending to a different address if they are withdrawing ETH.

Over the coming years, it's the ambition of DefiDomains to build out unified APIs that allow non-decentralized companies to easily integrate with DefiDomains for lookup and registration (so you can buy decentralized domains at your favorite registrar) and to launch our own traditional DNS servers to allow your average browser to resolve decentralized domains without costly blockchain transactions.

We have a long journey ahead of us, and a tough fight in taking on the largest centralized figure in the modern web. But we're excited to bring a new wave of decentralization to the world and win back the internet in a decentralized fashion!

Frequently Asked Questions

What is DefiDomains?

The DefiDomains system is an alternative to classic domain names and traditional DNS. Commonly referred to as an 'alternative DNS root'. DefiDomains offers:

  • NFT tokenized domain names on a central smart contract registry.
  • The ability to associate and resolve any type of information with an NFT token using a public resolver.
  • Multi-coin address resolution support.
  • Cross blockchain name resolution support.
  • Uncensorable domain names.

What is DefiDomains Inc?

DefiDomains Inc is the private company that is creating the DefiDomains protocol and acts as the first 'registrar'.

Registrars are companies that manage a specific TLD (like .defi) and allow users to register and manage their domains.

How is the DefiDomains system different from other alternative DNS roots?

Most alternative root systems only aim to replicate the DNS root that is owned by ICANN, a centralized foundation that was previously part of the US government. Some blockchain based alternative DNS roots such as Namecoin and Handshake attempt to fully replace ICANN's DNS system but do so on their own blockchains that require unique incentive methods and lack the hash security of the Ethereum blockchain.

The other main alternative domain system ENS (.eth). While ENS started as part of the Ethereum foundation it spun out into a seperate organization. ENS views themselves as part of the DNS global name space and is focused on resolving the .eth TLD, and existing TLDs. Not in bringing new systems to market. Furthermore, since the .eth TLD is already part of the gTLD namespace on traditional DNS, .eth can never be reconiciled in a system that supports traditional DNS.

The goal of DefiDomains on the other hand is to build a new system of TLDs that can eventually be bridged onto the main DNS space, through a specialized staking sidechain. Allowing users to use their new domains both in the same contexts they use .eth, but also eventually in traditional DNS. (It's super unlikely we'll ever be able to achieve such a system merge - but it's the dream)

While ENS is focused on bringing existing domain names into Ethereum, we're focused on going the other direction. Creating new TLDs as tokens, and bringing them to the larger world of DNS.

We think the work of ENS is critical (and its obviously foundational to our product as it is based on their work). We continue to support the work of ENS and do not see them as a competitor but instead a complementary service that deals with the other side of the same coin.

DefiDomains may never reach its ambition of full DNS alternative root status or merging into primary DNS, in which case we could continue to focus on providing unique alternative TLDs within blockchain ecosystems

What is DefiDomains view on the global DNS space?

We do not believe that ICANN/IANA should have sole anti-competitive authority over domain names, and that taking such a stance would fly in the face of the decentralization goals of blockchain technology.

At the same time, we understand and respect the complex and delicate system that DNS is and its importance to a functioning internet.

For this reason, DefiDomains is specifically bringing to market TLD extensions that do not currently exist in existing DNS name spaces.

We will respect the existing TLDs and gTLDs that ICANN has developed, and in turn expect the same.

This focus on supplemental TLDs is what will allow the DefiDomains system to eventually reconcile with traditional DNS systems

How do you plan to supply records to the main DNS space? (One day maybe?)

Our long term vision for this is to create a L2 network of validator nodes which provide validated records from the DefiDomains namespace in a standard DNS format, allowing them to be resolved by standard browsers and DNS services.

These validators will serve as API services allowing traditional vendors to access the DefiDomains namespace as well as allowing traditional registrars to DefiDomains EARNS names for sale.

This is similar to current sidechain forwarding protocols that we see in existing protocols like the Pokt network for general Ethereum transactions.

It should be noted that this goal is HIGHLY optimistic, long-term and unlikely. If for any reason that vision is part of why you want to acquire a Defi Domain - you should not purchase a DefiDomain.

Is there currently a DefiDomains token? And what is it for?

No - there is no current need for a token.

While one may make sense in the future for DNS staking, allowing companies to have their own TLDs, and for protocol governance. Those are bridges we are not ready to cross.

If/when that changes, we'll update this FAQ section and send out an email to our email list.

You should never purchase any token that claims to represent DefiDomains unless you've first checked to see if this FAQ question is updated.

How do I purchase a DefiDomains domain?

This website is configured to make purchasing a DefiDomains domain as simple as possible. When you type in a domain on the "register" tab it will automatically forward your transaction to the relevant "registrar" contract, as each TLD is controlled by a seperate and independent registrar.

When making a purchase, you'll first make a 'commitment' - this commitment is an encrypted confirmation of your intent to purchase a domain to prevent front-running and disputes.

After the commitment has been approved, you'll need to wait one minute before sending a transaction to finalize the registration.

These two seperate transactions are managed automatically on our website, but the time gap does not wait on a successful transaction response. So you will need to ensure you make a transaction using sufficient gas.

If you do not wish to use this site, you can directly interact with the registrar contract of your choice. You can look up these registrar on our "contracts" page. They have all been confirmed and supplied with an ABI on Etherscan making them easy to interact with.

Once I purchase a DefiDomains domain, what should I do to configure it?

Each DefiDomains domain uses its own independent resolver, to simplify partnership integrations and to allow for unique TLDs to have their own unique functionality in the future.

Once you've purchased your domain, you'll want to ensure two core pieces of resolver information are configured.

The first is setting your Ethereum address. Which can be done on the "Manage" page of our site, or by using the command "setAddr(node, address)" on the smart contract. (Note to developers: If you are integrating the resolver smart contract, please note that setAddr is an overloaded function with two versions. One for setting Ethereum addresses, and one for setting addresses for other tokens. To manage Ethereum addresses within an app using something like EthersJS you will need to declare the function explicitly as "setAddr(bytes32,address)")

The other parameter you'll want to configure is the domain name using the setName function. If you are doing this on the DefiDomains website, this second transaction will be done for you automatically.

This name function allows the resolver to return a simple plaintext string of the domain.

Can I currently use DefiDomains names in tools like MetaMask or exchanges?

As DefiDomains is in its Alpha launch, we currently have limited integrations. We're currently actively working with partners in order to be able to have DefiDomains names resolve in your favorite tools and will have updates for this shortly.

Why are some places unsafe to purchase DefiDomains names from and what should I look out for?

DefiDomains follows the namehash EIP-137 for name hashes which are non-human readable.

For this reason, ENS relies on resolvers to provide human readable strings to identify the specific name of a token.

When implemented properly, a DApp should resolve the human readable string from a resolver, and then apply the EIP-137 namehash to that string in order to ensure they match.

It is however possible for a DApp to improperly implement this system and to fail to check or validate a domain name, thus allowing for domain spoofing.

For this reason we encourage users to only purchase from the DefiDomains website, directly from contracts, or in the future from highly professional partners like OpenSea whom we are confident would have implemented the correct checks and vlaidations.

What does it cost to purchase an DefiDomains domain?

DefiDomains domains through our website are sold on an annualized basis.

Different TLDs have different price ranges, depending on if their target audience is commercial or personal use.

We also increase the price based on the length of the domain, meaning that shorter names cost more.

The price for different TLDs is outlined on the prices page of this website. But the annual renewal fee ranges from $10 - $1000+

This pricing range is intended to prevent domain squatting. Since the DefiDomains team is unable to takeback a domain after registration, the only way to prevent squatters is using fee incentives that would make squatting without using a domain less likely to be a lucrative experience.

Is there a limit to the domain name length I can register like with the initial ENS offering?

Unlike with the initial ENS offering, there is currently no reserve or block on registering shortnames. Any user, so long as they pay the fee, may register a short name.

Are there auctions taking place for speciality names?

No. The team has not pre-registered any domains. All domains will be available first come, first serve.

Any domains appearing in auctions, are ones purchased by community members.

Can I get my own TLD?

DefiDomains supports the ability to create custom TLDs for projects and partners, however, the creation and on-going management of these TLDs does include a signifigant cost.

Partners interested in a custom TLD can reach out via DM to @adamscochran on Twitter.

Partners whom reach more than 100,000+ users, may apply to have a custom TLD without payment, that DefiDomains will manage, so long as users are billed for the TLD. Affiliate arrangements and revenue sharing are available for such partners.

Does DefiDomains support non-English, non-ASCII and emoji characters?

Yes. DefiDomains supports all character types since all names are standardized and in turn stored as a hash. In theory, you could even set a picture as a domain name.

However, many applications may be unable to interact with DefiDomains domains that do not use standard characters.

In most cases, English, Spanish, German, Latin, Russian and Chinese character sets are supported, and emojis are supported via punycode.

The DefiDomains website itself does not currently guarentee functionality when working with non-English characters. But, all punycode encoded icons should work.

I'm a partner interested in integrating DefiDomains, how should I get started?

Partners who have already listed ENS will have no problem integrating the DefiDomains system.

DefiDomains has its own primary registry contract that can be used to identify the relevant resolvers for each TLD. Each TLD in turn has its own unique registrar and resolver.

With ENS you likely integrated with the public resolver and ETH registrar controller. Our system uses an almost identical ABI, and simple has different resolver/registrars based on the TLD.

If you haven't yet integrated with ENS and are looking for support in integrating DefiDomains, please reach out to @adamscochran on Twitter

I represent a different blockchain can you integrate DefiDomains with our chain?

If you are an EVM based blockchain that supports solidity smart contracts, we'd be happy to integrate DefiDomains support onto your blockchain. Such teams can reach out to @adamscochran on Twitter to discuss grant funding costs for such integrations.

If you are a non-EVM based blockchain, we can likely still offer some limited functionality in the future when our DefiDomains DNS API sidechain is available. Please reach out and let us know of your interest and budget so we can support.

How is DefiDomains governed?

Currently DefiDomains is managed by a privately held entity.

However, the DefiDomains system is configured in such a manner that it is not possible for the team to seize, edit, reclaim, modify or overwrite your domain name once you own it as long as you continue to make your annual payments.

In the future we want to explore a method of decentralized governance for the system - but do not feel we yet have a sound model in place to do that responsibly.

Is this just an ENS fork? Is it open source?

The core of the DefiDomains system is a fork of ENS whose source code can be viewed at anytime on Etherscan.

Unlike ENS, the DefiDomains system is designed to support multiple new TLDs.

While we hope to progress in our own development, we'll also continue to contribute both financially and in open-source, where we can, to ENS, as it is a critical project that made ours possible.

Do you support ENS?

We love ENS, it was the inspiration for what we are building. For that reason we've committed to giving a portion of our annual revenue as a grant to the non-profit foundation for ENS.

We are also working on ways to support the resolving of .eth domains within our system, so that ENS can benefit from our future work.

What are your view on forking? Is it fair for you to make money from someone else's code?

While Defidomains is based on ENS, there are small code changes, signifigant configuration/deployment changes, and a new (albiet quite ugly and clunky) frontend - as well as the support for new TLDs.

We believe a company is ultimately more than just its code - but more importantly we believe that open-source is super important, even in commercial environments as it drives innovation.

ENS has released their code under the BSD-2 license which allows for commercial use, modification, distribution and private use. This is one of the most open and permissive licenses.

If open source projects want to disclose source-code without granting rights to it, they do not have to provide a license or can provide custom restrictive licenses. (Although that gets pretty gross)

That said we realize the importance, and cost, of the work that origonal developers do, and that's why we are dedicating a portion of our revnue as grants to continue to support ENS (as long as they'll take it) and to other open source projects via Gitcoin Grants

We also continue to build on the work ENS has done, and make our own work open source under similar licenses. So if someone seeks to make money of the work we do - that's great too.

We hope through any means, we can provide economic opportunities for individuals to pursue innovation in the decentralized space!

What is your commitment to Gitcoin grants?

Starting with the first 2021 grant cycle, we'll contribute a minimum of 10% of our revenue to Gitcoin grants, with 5% going to the matching pool and 5% in contributions from our team.

Do you support payment methods other than ETH?

No - not at this time.

We are working on a custom registrat implement with a WordPress Woocommerce shop that would allow users to make recurring purchases for their domain via credit card, as well as support payments of multiple different tokens.

Tests have been going well for this in testnet environments but there are additional challenges to overcome to ensure high enough levels of payment security that our payment partners would be comfortable in rolling out.

What happens if there is a bug or issue in the code that needs updating?

This is a serious risk.

We'll make all attempts to work with the community and other stakeholders to create new versions of the contract without any such issues and migrate domains over to these new contracts.

It is important to note however that these challenges may require unrealistic turnarounds, may be out of technical capabilities of the team, or could be unresolvable, so purchasing any such NFT does carry risk that the team may not be able to deploy a fix or solution.

We may also be up against a challenge in which the onchain record has changed from the exploit and we'd need to use offchain records (such as a graph-node) for allowing people to migrate and claim their names.

While we believe these situations to be unlikely, it's important to disclaim that these risks and concerns do exist.
